The Stakes for PMC

The Pune Municipal Corporation governs one of India's fastest-growing and most strategically significant cities — home to a major IT industry cluster, premier educational institutions, one of the country's largest defence establishments, and a rapidly expanding urban population. PMC administers public health through Sassoon General Hospital and its network of dispensaries, manages property and infrastructure across a vast urban and peri-urban footprint, and serves citizens through initiatives like PMC Connect and the Smart City Mission.

As PMC deepens its digital transformation — already one of Maharashtra's most ambitious — the volume of sensitive data in play grows with every service made online. Citizen health records, property assessments, defence-adjacent infrastructure data, student records, tax filings: all of it demands the highest standard of data governance.

Most commercial AI tools in use today — chatbots, document summarisers, translation services, coding assistants — are powered by servers in the United States or China. Every query sent to these tools carries the risk of transmitting sensitive PMC data across international borders, outside the jurisdiction of Indian law and entirely beyond PMC's administrative control.

Where Does Your Data Go Today?

When a PMC officer uses a mainstream AI assistant to draft a notice, analyse a tender document, or summarise a health report, that text — along with any embedded data — travels over the public internet to servers owned by US or Chinese technology corporations. This is not a theoretical risk. It is the default architecture of every major cloud AI platform.

For a city with Pune's profile — IT hub, defence presence, dense civic data — the consequences of a data exposure incident go well beyond administrative embarrassment. They carry national security implications.

Why Xarka for PMC

The question for PMC is not whether to adopt AI — the operational efficiency gains are undeniable, and smart city peers globally are already realising them. Pune's own Smart City Mission is evidence that the Corporation has the appetite and capability to lead on technology. The question is how to adopt AI in a way that does not create new risks for the citizens PMC is constitutionally obligated to serve.

Foreign AI providers offer privacy assurances through terms of service. Xarka offers privacy through architecture. Because the model runs inside PMC's infrastructure, it is physically incapable of transmitting data to any external party — regardless of what legal changes occur in the US or China, regardless of data breach incidents at foreign companies, and regardless of geopolitical developments that might put Indian government data at risk.

Pune is not merely a city — it is the intellectual and strategic heartland of Maharashtra, home to institutions that shape India's future. The AI systems that serve it must be as trustworthy, as local, and as accountable as the institution they support.

Choosing indigenous Indian AI is also an act of national self-determination. Every rupee invested in Xarka's Indian LLM builds capability that stays in India, creates technology jobs in India, and ensures that the AI layer governing public services reflects Indian values, Indian languages, and Indian law — not Silicon Valley priorities or Beijing's strategic interests.
